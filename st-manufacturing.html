MANUFACTURING

Another sector of air travel affected by COVID-19 includes manufacturing, consisting of building, designing, and shipping airplanes, drones, and other air transportation vehicles across the world for use. Two main competitors in the airplane industry include Boeing and Airbus, both of whom are described in detail below, and looking at drones, KittyHawk and Joby Aviation are the prime competitors in this industry. Due to COVID-19, a majority of air transportation vehicle manufacturers have been met with severe losses which are explored in this section.

The GCAMI’s Growth Prior to COVID-19

The Global Commercial Aircraft Manufacturing Industry (GCAMI) produces a complete civilian aircraft, including aerospace engines, propulsion units, auxiliary equipment, and parts.

  • From 2014 to 2019, the industry has grown by 1.7%

    • Increase in passenger travel and air travel

  • Growing demand for fuel-efficient aircraft and rising global air travel

Boeing

Boeing is one of the leading airplane manufacturing companies in the world, but due to the extreme effects of COVID-19, Boeing has experienced a dramatic loss in revenue, compared to its first quarter in 2019:

  • 26% decrease in the company’s overall revenue

    • 22,917 million dollars in 2019 to 16,908 million dollars in 2020

  • 66% decrease in commercial plane deliveries

    • Commercial airplane deliveries have dropped from 149 deliveries in the first quarter of 2019 to 50 in the first quarter of 2020.

  • Commercial airplane revenue has plummeted a staggering 48% as a result.

  • As of April 29th, 2020, Boeing reports a total backlog of 439 billion dollars

  • Significant decreases in revenue are due to reduced airline passenger traffic and an unstable supply chain.
